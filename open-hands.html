Tools and Materials

  • Aluminum foil, approx. 10 x 10 inches.
  • Corrugated cardboard, 6 x 10 inches (not too thick).
  • 23 small signal diodes.
  • Small jewellry crimp beads.
  • A short length of ethernet cable.
  • A glue stick.
  • A craft knife, straight-edge, and cutting mat.
  • Scissors.
  • Needlenose pliers (for crimping connectors).
  • Tweezers (for wrapping foil around wires).

Assembly Instructions

  1. Print the first three pattern pages on paper. Glue aluminum foil to the two foil pages. You don't have to use the entire sheet of paper, but leave enough extra around the edges to hold onto when you're cutting out the pieces later. Glue the spacer page to a sheet of corrugated cardboard.
  2. Print the other two pattern pages on card stock. Cut out the black parts on the first one to allow the key tabs to flex.
  3. Take the backing card and its associated foil page. Cut out the foil pieces. Start with the innermost one and work your way outwards. Trim the inside edge of each piece before you cut it off the sheet so you're not working on the inside edge of a tiny skinny piece of material. Glue the pieces to the card, paper side down, foil side showing.
  4. Take a piece of ethernet cable. Choose six wires and strip both ends. Cut the other two wires off short. Crimp the wires to the foil pieces, writing the wire colors on the chart to the right.
  5. Take the key card and its associated foil page. Cut out the foil pieces and glue the paper side to the card.
  6. Connect each key to its connector bar with a diode and two crimp connectors. The marked end of the diode should point toward the key.
  7. Take the other piece of ethernet cable, strip four of the wires and cut off the other four. Crimp them to the connector bars, writing the wire colors on the chart to the right.
  8. Cut out the spacer (the dark bits below the bottom keys are angled ramps for your thumbs). Cut grooves for the cables to exit.
  9. Cut the backing card to size and glue all three layers together. Cut flaps on the top card to fold around and finish the edges.
  10. Connect the wires to pins 2-11 of an Arduino Uno.
